About Lottopsy

Lottopsy is an interactive viewer and picker of lottery numbers.

Use Lottopsy to dissect the multidimensional lottery number space. Slice up sections of the lottery number space, and find lottery numbers that belong to those slices.

Plots

Each plot displays lottery numbers two dimensions at a time. Change plots by clicking the lottery balls on the plot axes. Within a plot, click on a point to show corresponding draws. Larger points indicate multiple draws at that point.

Slices

Click the slice button to enter slice mode. In slice mode, connect points on the plot grid to form a shape (slice). You may create multiple slices. Slices determine the success or failure of quick picks.

Quick Picks

Click the quick pick button to generate a quick pick, a random set of numbers. A quick pick is labeled "passed" only if it falls inside of at least one slice in every plot. Plots in which you defined no slices do not count (you can think of these plots as one giant slice). "Failed" quick picks will report the dimensions in which it falls outside of a slice.

Game

Choose a lottery game from the dropdown at the top of the page. Changing games will reset all slices and quick picks.

Date

Drag the lower or upper handle of the date slider to control which lottery draws appear on the plots. Lock the date slider to keep the number of draws constant while dragging.

Trajectory

Turn on the trajectory switch to show lines connecting lottery draws in the order they occurred.
